Data Use Card

A measurable contract for AI-ready data
Russell Dudek · Candidate for VP, Data Platform & Knowledge

Distinctive Thesis

Data trust should be issued, not inferred.A priority dataset should not enter AI use until its identity, quality, meaning, freshness, ownership, approved uses, restrictions, and issue-service expectations are legible to people and machines.

The Operating Mechanism

BehaviorRequired outputDecision made easier
IdentifySource, domain, owner, authoritative fields, consumers, critical decisionsWhat is this dataset, and who is accountable?
QualifyQuality score, freshness, lineage, semantic clarity, privacy/security, fitnessCan this use case rely on it?
PublishDiscoverable terms, approved uses, restrictions, examples, machine-readable metadataCan a team choose it without escalation?
ServiceDetection, escalation, owner, SLA, remediation, deprecation behaviorWhat happens when it fails?
LearnUsage, questions, exceptions, model outcomes, rework, version changesShould the standard expand, change, or retire?

Decision Rights

Platform leadership decidesMinimum metadata and quality contract; architecture standards; prioritization; observability; source onboarding requirements; exception and deprecation policy.
Domain owners decideAuthoritative meaning; acceptable use; business thresholds; remediation ownership; human authority; product-specific consequences.

Balanced Scorecard

DimensionPrimary measureLeading signal
Trust coverage% priority datasets rated and documentedOwner and contract completeness
Service reliabilityDetection-to-remediation timeIssue age and escalation clarity
Onboarding speedScope-to-production cycle timeReusable steps and regression rate
Self-serviceAd hoc questions escalated to platform teamSuccessful discovery and source selection
AI outcome qualityUse-case-specific error, abstention, rework, and adoptionTerms coverage and exception learning

Change Burden

Source owners must maintain meaning, quality, and service obligations; AI teams must select data through published terms rather than tribal knowledge; engineering must expose lineage, incidents, and quality telemetry; executives must support explicit holds when the evidence is insufficient. Adoption is not a communications plan - it is the new default way work enters the platform.
