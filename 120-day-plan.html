Starting Principle

Do not begin with a platform reorganization or abstract governance council. Begin with active decisions, priority data, visible quality, named ownership, one net-new source, and two AI teams that need to act.

Days 0-30 · Baseline the Trust System

Executive alignment

Define the operating outcome

Confirm the business decisions, AI use cases, priority domains, risk boundaries, and C-suite expectations that make this platform strategically important.

  • Agree on priority dataset definition
  • Clarify decision rights and escalation authority
  • Name executive sponsors and domain owners
Platform reality

Read the architecture and flow

Inventory ingestion, storage, transformation, vector/embedding pipelines, catalogs, semantic layers, observability, access, incidents, and technical debt.

  • Map lineage and failure points
  • Identify committed vs. open architecture choices
  • Assess team capability and operating cadence
Trust baseline

Quantify the starting state

Baseline quality coverage, documentation completeness, issue age, remediation time, source onboarding cycle, and escalated data questions.

  • Select initial priority datasets
  • Define measurement units and baselines
  • Label unknowns rather than inventing maturity scores
Use-case focus

Choose the first proof portfolio

Select one net-new source and two active AI teams where the result is important, measurable, and representative enough to teach the enterprise.

  • Choose bounded, decision-critical use cases
  • Identify human authority and risk limits
  • Capture baseline question and rework volume

Days 61-90 · Prove the Mechanism

Net-new source

Scope to production in 30 days or fewer

Run the complete onboarding playbook, validate zero regression to existing pipelines, capture effort, exceptions, decisions, and reusable components.

  • Regression and rollback evidence
  • Named owner and service terms
  • Post-onboarding learning review
AI team 1

Independent source selection

Enable one team to discover and choose data using published terms, examples, restrictions, and quality posture rather than platform-team interpretation.

  • Measure escalated questions
  • Observe decision latency and rework
  • Capture missing metadata or semantics
AI team 2

Repeat in a different domain

Test whether the standard transfers across a different data shape, product context, risk boundary, or semantic need.

  • Compare reusable vs. domain-specific fields
  • Verify human authority
  • Refine the discovery experience
Quality service

Exercise the SLA

Use real or controlled quality events to verify detection, escalation, ownership, remediation, communication, and incident learning.

  • Track age and handoffs
  • Expose unowned failure modes
  • Correct the mechanism, not only the incident

Risks to Manage

False precisionDo not manufacture a maturity score before definitions, baselines, and owners are agreed.
Catalog theaterMetadata without trusted meaning, issue service, and actual use will not create self-sufficiency.
Centralization dragDo not move domain knowledge into a central bottleneck; make the contract common and ownership distributed.
AI urgency bypassDo not let business pressure turn undocumented data into production assumptions that become expensive to unwind.

Day-120 Executive Readout

A credible readout should show what Wolfe can now trust, what teams can now do independently, what source onboarding taught, where issue service still fails, what architectural decisions are required, what team is needed, and what the next 12-24 months should fund. The result is not a governance presentation. It is a measurable operating system for AI-ready data.
