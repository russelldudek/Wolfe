Data Use Card Standard

Illustrative operating artifact · Russell Dudek
Candidate for VP, Data Platform & Knowledge · https://russelldudek.github.io/Wolfe/

Make the terms visible before AI use.

A Data Use Card is a compact human- and machine-readable contract that tells a team what a dataset is, whether it is fit for a use, who owns it, what it may do, and what happens when it fails.

Illustrative purpose
Demonstrate the operating mechanism. Final fields, thresholds, tooling, and decisions require Wolfe discovery and architecture review.

Minimum Contract

IdentityDataset name, domain, source systems, version, authoritative fields, lifecycle state.
OwnershipData product owner, source steward, technical owner, issue-remediation owner.
MeaningDefinitions, units, examples, semantic relationships, known ambiguity, change history.
QualityDimensions, score, critical-field coverage, freshness, completeness, duplicate and reconciliation rules.
Use TermsApproved AI and analytical uses, prohibited uses, restrictions, required joins, intended decisions.
AuthorityHuman decision rights, abstention/escalation rules, access and entitlement boundaries.
ServiceDetection, severity, escalation path, remediation SLA, communication, rollback/deprecation.
EvidenceUsage, questions, incidents, model outcomes, rework, adoption, learning and next review.

Operating Rule

No invisible assumptions.A dataset may be technically accessible and still be unfit for an AI use. The platform should make an approved, conditional, or hold posture explicit - with the reason and the next responsible action.

Issuance and Service

How the Data Use Card becomes platform behavior
Russell Dudek · Candidate for VP, Data Platform & Knowledge

Issuance Path

StageRequired workExit evidence
RegisterName the source, owner, critical consumers, decisions, dependencies, sensitivity, and current state.Dataset identity and accountability are explicit.
AssessEvaluate quality, freshness, lineage, semantic clarity, access, security, privacy, and fitness for the selected use.Risks, unknowns, and required controls are visible.
ContractDefine approved uses, restrictions, human authority, issue service, examples, and version behavior.Terms are reviewable by domain, platform, security, and AI stakeholders.
PublishExpose the card in catalog, semantic search, developer workflow, and machine-readable metadata.AI teams can discover and interpret it without private explanation.
ObserveMonitor data quality, questions, use, incidents, model outcomes, adoption, and new semantic conflict.Terms remain current and useful.
Renew or retireReissue after material change; hold or retire when ownership, evidence, or service fails.Stale trust does not remain production truth.

Posture Logic

Approved

Use within published terms

Required identity, quality, meaning, ownership, authority, and service conditions are met for the named use.

Conditional

Use with explicit control

A known gap is bounded by a human checkpoint, restriction, use-case limit, or temporary service control.

Hold

Do not use yet

Authority, quality, semantic meaning, access, or remediation ownership is insufficient for the proposed use.

Retire

Remove from active selection

The dataset is superseded, stale, unowned, noncompliant, or no longer able to meet its published service contract.

Issue Service Contract

QuestionRequired answer
How is failure detected?Quality rule, reconciliation, freshness monitor, usage feedback, incident, model behavior, or owner report.
Who owns response?Named source steward, technical owner, domain owner, and executive escalation.
What is the clock?Severity-specific detection, acknowledgment, containment, remediation, and communication targets.
Who must know?Affected AI/product teams, business owners, security/legal/finance where relevant, and downstream consumers.
What changes afterward?Root cause, card terms, scoring, observability, onboarding standard, or architecture - not merely the ticket status.

First Pilot Review

Use one net-new source and two AI teams.Measure onboarding cycle, regression, question volume, source-selection success, issue age, rework, and the specific fields teams still need. The standard earns scale by reducing uncertainty in real work.
