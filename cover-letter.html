Wolfe's challenge is not simply to ingest more data. It is to make trust visible enough that AI teams can act without repeatedly asking the platform organization which source is safe, current, authoritative, and fit for a specific use. Your published measures make the mandate unusually concrete: rate and document priority datasets, remediate quality issues in under 24 hours, onboard a new source in 30 days or fewer, and cut escalated data questions by half.

My career has repeatedly centered on converting complex technical systems into accountable operating mechanisms. At Vape-Jet, I connect production, quality, support, customer, engineering, ERP, CRM, telemetry, and machine-fleet signals into clearer priorities, handoffs, standard work, and learning loops. Through DudeWorth, I design AI-readiness and agentic workflow patterns around data readiness, retrieval, evaluation, human judgment, governance, and adoption. At Compunetics, I led engineering, manufacturing, quality, and customer-delivery systems in high-reliability environments. At Amazon, I built visible routines, escalation paths, and standard work in a 24/7 launch environment supporting approximately five million second-day orders annually.

That combination shapes my thesis for Wolfe: every AI decision should know the terms of its data. I would make those terms explicit through a Data Use Card - a machine- and human-readable contract covering source identity, semantic meaning, quality, freshness, ownership, approved uses, restrictions, and issue service levels. The purpose is not another governance artifact. It is a platform behavior that makes quality measurable, onboarding repeatable, and self-service credible.

My background is broader than a career spent inside one data-engineering function. That breadth is valuable here because Wolfe is asking this leader to align AI product, engineering, operations, security, business leadership, and source owners around one standard while building a team capable of moving with founder-level urgency. I am comfortable working directly with technical specialists, making high-consequence tradeoffs legible in business terms, and holding the operating system - not merely the presentation - accountable to results.

The accompanying candidate vision applies this reasoning to Wolfe's published targets and includes a 120-day proof plan plus the Data Use Card mechanism. The complete URL is https://russelldudek.github.io/Wolfe/. I would welcome a discussion about where uncertainty is currently blocking Wolfe's AI teams: data access, semantic meaning, quality, ownership, or the speed of remediation.
